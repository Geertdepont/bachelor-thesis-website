mvvm

  • Guideline 1: Models, Views, and ViewModels should exclusively expose their state
  • Guideline 2: The app should possess a single source of truth of data.
  • Guideline 3: Models should be evolvable/testable
  • Guideline 4: ViewModels should not refer to View-specific components.
  • Guideline 5: Views should always know about changes after ViewModels
  • Guideline 6: Adopt one Model for each feature of the app.
  • Guideline 7: Keep ViewModels as simple as possible.
  • Guideline 8: The state of the app should be defined in the Models only.
  • Guideline 9: The data produced by the Models should be reliable and of high quality.
  • Guideline 10: Networking or data access functionalities should be performed by Models.

Keep ViewModels as simple as possible. When needed, transfer responsibility to other layers.


Why?

Delegating responsibilities from the ViewModels to other layers results into smaller and more understandable ViewModels. It also makes testing the whole architecture easier. Since each component can be tested separately. Instead of asserting that a View needs to behave after a certain retrieval from the model, the model now can be tested independently.
